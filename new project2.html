Golden Temple

The Golden Temple is the holiest site in Sikhism, also known as Sri Harmandir Sahib, located in Amritsar, India. It is famous for its gold-plated structure and is a symbol of spiritual and communal harmony, openness, and equality due to its four entrances

Varanasi temple

Varanasi's most prominent temple is the Shri Kashi Vishwanath Temple, dedicated to Lord Shiva and one of the twelve most sacred Jyotirlingas. Located on the western bank of the River Ganga, it is also known as the "Golden Temple" due to the gold plating on its spire, a donation from Maharaja Ranjit Singh.
